What is Rappelling?

Rappelling, also known as abseiling, is an extreme sport that involves descending steep cliffs or rock faces using a controlled rope system. It is an exciting and challenging activity that requires technique, courage, and proper safety equipment. Whether you're exploring canyons, waterfalls, or rock formations, rappelling offers a thrilling way to connect with nature while testing your limits.

Risks of Rappelling

While rappelling is an adrenaline-pumping experience, it carries risks such as rope failure, improper anchoring, or losing control during descent. To ensure safety, participants should receive proper training, use certified equipment, and follow expert instructions. The most common risks include falls, rope burns, and unpredictable weather conditions.
